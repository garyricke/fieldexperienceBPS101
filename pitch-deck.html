The Case for Change

Why Change? — The Students

The World of Work Is Changing

AI and automation are reshaping entire industries. Students need real-world exposure to understand what careers actually look like — not just what they imagine.

The Cost of Getting It Wrong

Post-secondary education is expensive. Students can't afford career or major changes after investing years and tuition. A student who "loves engineering" may discover it's not the right fit — better to learn at 17 than at 22.

Not Every Path Goes Through College

For students where direct-to-college isn't the right path, work-based learning provides structured, meaningful alternatives with real credentials and experience.

Decisions Based on Minimal Experience

Students are choosing life paths based on a class or two. Field Experience lets them try a career before committing — seeing if the day-to-day reality matches their expectations.

"Try now to see if it's the right fit."

The Course

Field Experience — Redesigned

We're redesigning the 20-year-old Link course to meet all current requirements — state guidelines, CCPE mandates, and the 91-page ISBE Work-Based Learning manual.

The new Field Experience course offers four distinct types of work-based learning, each tailored to different student needs and career pathways.

Course Structure

Boot Camp (2–3 weeks) covering employability skills, professional expectations, and workplace readiness — followed by placement in one of the four experience types.

Student-Led Experiences

Incubator entrepreneurs continuing and growing their own business ventures

School-Based Experiences

School store, BHS merchandise operations, Bulldog Media Team, and other in-building enterprises

Clinical Experiences

Medically focused placements through BNAT and healthcare pathway partners

Internship Experiences

60+ hours with community employers — building both employability and technical skills

Experience Types

Four Pathways, One Course

01

Student-Led

Incubator entrepreneurs who continue developing and operating their own business. Students apply business plans created in prior coursework to real-world operations.

02

School-Based

Operating the school store, managing BHS merchandise, and running the Bulldog Media Team. Real business operations within a supported school environment.

03

Clinical

Medically focused placements through the BNAT program and healthcare partners. Students gain hands-on clinical experience in healthcare settings.

04

Internships

60+ hours with community employers building employability skills and technical competencies. The cornerstone of the CCPE supervised career development requirement.

All four types include the Boot Camp foundation (2–3 weeks) covering professional readiness, communication, and workplace expectations.

Call to Action

BPS Should Model the Way

We're asking the community to host interns. BPS needs to lead by example — offering internship opportunities across our own departments.

Why Internal Placements Matter

  • Equity — Not all students have transportation to off-site placements
  • Supervision — School-based placements offer built-in support structures
  • Modeling — If we ask the community to participate, we should participate first

Every department should consider what an internship could look like.
